Echo Speaker

Type

Personal project

Role

UX/UI Designer

Tool(s)

Figma, Adobe Photoshop

Overview

Echo Speaker is a fictional brand generated through a Random Project Generator as part of my Figma Essentials online course. Guided by a detailed project brief, I designed a website for the brand, incorporating skills and techniques learned throughout the course. The brief challenged me to design an e-commerce website tailored to a specific demographic.

The goal 🎯

My goal was to design a speaker brand website page for its latest collection, prioritizing a user-centered approach that catered to the specific needs, preferences, and behaviors of the brand's target audience.

Defining the Brief

User Persona

John (43, Married, Part-timer, Jakarta) - John and his wife use music daily to unwind after long commutes, valuing high-quality sound and attractive speaker designs. Heâ€™s seeking an affordable, premium-looking speaker to replace his old one but finds online shopping overwhelming due to cluttered interfaces and untrustworthy sites. While John prioritizes sound quality and detailed product information, his wife focuses on aesthetics like design and color.

User Flow

To provide a clearer understanding of how the user interacts and navigates through the website, I developed a detailed user flow.

The Design Process

Low Fidelity Wireframes 📄

At the beginning of the redesign process, I started by creating a basic black-and-white wireframe in Figma, prioritizing the layout and structure over colors and visual details.

Style Guide 🎈

For the design, I opted for a clean and minimalist approach to create a sense of exclusivity and a premium feel for the site. To reinforce the clean and minimalist design, I chose a monochromatic color palette. The palette features shades of gray, ranging from pure white to deep charcoal, creating a sleek and cohesive look.

This choice emphasizes simplicity while giving the site a modern, premium feel. The lighter shades are used to maintain a sense of openness and clarity, while the darker tones add contrast and depth, enhancing the visual hierarchy and drawing attention to key elements.

Hi-Fi Wireframe 📱

After completing the low-fidelity wireframe, I moved on to refining the design and incorporating colors. As mentioned earlier, I maintained a clean and minimalist aesthetic throughout the design. For the product listing page, I implemented a feature that allows users to preview different product colors without navigating away or switching to another page. Additionally, I included a 'limited edition' label for select products to enhance the brand's sense of exclusivity.

On the checkout, payment, & review page, I added a progress indicator at the top to improve user experience. This feature helps users understand where they are in the process and what steps lie ahead, ensuring a smoother and more transparent checkout journey.
